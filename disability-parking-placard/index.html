1. What Is a Disability Parking Placard?

A placard or special license plate allows you to park in designated accessible spaces, park for free at meters, and ignore time limits in many states. It is issued to the person, not the vehicle, and can be used in any car you are driving or riding in.

Two types
  • Permanent – valid 2–6 years (varies by state), no fee in most states
  • Temporary – 3–12 months, usually $5–$15

2. Who Qualifies?

Typical qualifying conditions (varies slightly by state):

  • Cannot walk 200 feet without stopping to rest
  • Requires portable oxygen
  • Severe cardiac, lung, or neurological condition
  • Lost use of lower extremities or both hands
  • Legal blindness or severely limited vision

Only a **licensed medical professional** (MD, DO, NP, PA, chiropractor, optometrist) can certify – we give you the **doctor-letter template** inside the PDF.

3. Four-Step Universal Process

  1. Download your state form (links in table below)
  2. Doctor completes certification (template in PDF)
  3. Submit to DMV – online, mail, or in-person
  4. Receive placard – 7-30 days depending on state

6. Online vs Mail vs In-Person – Pros & Cons

  • Online (CA, FL partial): 5 min, instant confirmation, but limited to renewals in some states
  • Mail: slow (2-4 weeks), but no appointment needed – **certified-mail proof** included in PDF

7. FAQ – Everything Google Keeps Asking

How long does it take to receive the placard?

CA: 2-4 weeks, TX: 7-10 days, FL: 10-14 days, NY: 14-21 days.

Can I use my placard in another state?

Yes – all states recognize valid out-of-state placards under interstate reciprocity.

Can I get a placard for a temporary condition (broken leg)?

Yes – choose “temporary” on the form. Valid 3-12 months depending on state.

Is there a fee?

Permanent: free in 45 states. Temporary: $5–$25 (see table).

Can I apply online?

Only CA and FL (some counties) allow new applications online. Others require mail or in-person.

Can undocumented immigrants apply?

Yes – no SSN required. Use ITIN or passport number on form.

Can I get 2 placards?

Most states allow **one permanent + one temporary** or **two temporary** if you have two cars.

What if my placard is lost or stolen?

File REG 156 (CA) or equivalent – replacement is free for permanent, fee for temporary.

Can I dispute a denial?

Yes – submit additional medical documentation or request administrative review (address in PDF).

Can I renew online?

CA: yes. TX, FL, NY: mail only. Renewal permanent **does not** require new doctor letter in most states.
