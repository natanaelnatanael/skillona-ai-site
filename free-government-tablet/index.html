1. What Is the Emergency Connectivity Fund (ECF)?

ECF is a **$7.17 billion federal program** created during COVID to provide **free laptops, tablets, and internet** to students and library patrons. **Tablets are 100 % free** – no monthly fees, no credit check, no deposit. Program runs **until 2026**.

Key facts

You do **not** have to be a student – **seniors, unemployed, SNAP recipients** can qualify through public libraries. Device is yours to keep.

2. Who Qualifies?

Any of the following = **automatic approval** (2025 limits):

  • Household income ≤ **200 % FPL** (≈ $29 160 single, $60 000 family of 4)
  • Participation in **SNAP, Medicaid, SSI, WIC, Federal Pell Grant, Section 8, VA Pension, TANF, LIHEAP, free/reduced lunch
  • Enrolled in **K-12, college, adult education, or public library** that receives ECF
  • Undocumented immigrants welcome – **no SSN required** (ITIN or self-certification)

3. Four-Step Universal Process

  1. Find your state provider (table below)
  2. Upload proof (SNAP card, Medicaid letter, pay stub, tax return, school ID)
  3. Choose tablet model (iPad 9, Samsung Tab A8, or Lenovo 10″)
  4. Receive by mail – 5-10 business days, tracking provided

6. Required Documents – Checklist

Proof of eligibility (any ONE):
  • SNAP card (photo both sides)
  • Medicaid approval letter
  • SSI benefit statement
  • Most recent tax return (first page only)
  • School letter (free/reduced lunch or enrollment)
Identity: driver's license, state ID, passport, or ITIN card.
Address: utility bill, lease, or bank statement (last 30 days).

7. Application Screenshots & Tips

  • Upload **clear photos** (not scans) – systems auto-reject blurry images
  • File size ≤ 5 MB – rename: snap_card.jpg
  • Choose **Wi-Fi model** if you already have hotspot – ships faster
  • Screenshot confirmation page – contains **tracking number**

8. What Happens After You Apply?

  1. Provider verifies documents within **3-5 business days**
  2. You receive **approval email** with tracking number
  3. Tablet ships via **FedEx Ground** – signature required
  4. Device is **yours to keep** – no return, no monthly fee
  5. You can **re-apply every 3 years** (new ECF window)
Important

**Do not pay anyone** – ECF devices are 100 % free. If a site asks for payment, it's a scam.

10. FAQ – Everything Google Keeps Asking

How long does it take to receive the tablet?

5-10 business days after approval. Human-I-T ships fastest (CA, NY, PA).

Can I choose the model?

Yes – iPad 9, Samsung Tab A8, or Lenovo 10″ (stock dependent).

Can I get more than one tablet?

One per **household** per 3-year window. Separate address = separate household.

Can I sell the tablet?

Legally yes – device is yours. Morally: donate if you no longer need it.

Do I need to return it?

No. ECF devices are **gifts**, not loans.

Can seniors apply?

Yes – through public library ECF program (same income limits).

Can I apply if I already have a phone?

Yes – owning a phone does not disqualify you.

What if I am denied?

Upload clearer documents or choose another provider – some have looser rules.

Is there a credit check?

No. ECF is **not** Lifeline – no credit, no deposit, no contract.

Can I apply from Puerto Rico?

Yes – ECF covers all US territories.
