How to legally find and claim unclaimed money held by US states: bank accounts, checks, insurance, refunds and more. Where to search, what to prepare and how to complete claims.

1. What Is Unclaimed Property?

Unclaimed property is money or assets that belong to individuals or businesses but have been turned over to a US state because the owner could not be contacted.

Examples:

  • Old bank accounts or CDs
  • Uncashed checks (payroll, refunds, dividends)
  • Utility deposits
  • Insurance payouts
  • Safe deposit box contents
Key point

States hold these funds as custodians. You can search and claim them directly, without paying a percentage to third parties.

2. Where to Search (Official Sources)

Most US states participate in official unclaimed property portals. Common places to start:

  • National-level search portals (multi-state)
  • Individual state treasurer or comptroller websites
  • Special sites for federal refunds (IRS, pensions, etc.)

Search using your full name, former names, and old addresses. If you owned or managed a company, search under the business name as well.

3. Step-by-Step Search Process

Step 1 – Make a list of name/address combinations

For each person or business you want to search, list:

  • Full legal name and possible variations
  • Previous surnames (marriage, etc.)
  • Addresses in different states where they lived or worked

Step 2 – Search by state

Many portals allow multi-state search, but also check individual state sites where you had the strongest connections (residence, job, bank).

Step 3 – Record each potential match

For each promising record, note:

  • State
  • Holder name (bank, employer, insurer, etc.)
  • Approximate amount range (if shown)
  • Claim or property ID number

4. Documents Typically Needed for Claims

Requirements vary by state, but common documents include:

  • Government ID (passport, driver’s license, ID card)
  • Proof of current address
  • Proof of past address (old bill, statement, letter)
  • Proof of relationship (if claiming for a deceased relative or business)
  • Company documents (if claiming under a business name)

Some states allow uploading scans online; others require notarized forms or mail.

5. How to Complete and Submit a Claim

General pattern:

  • Click on the record → “Claim” or similar button
  • Fill in contact information and relationship to the property
  • Upload or mail required documentation
  • Sign the form (sometimes in front of a notary)

Always follow the exact instructions on the state’s site – they have legal authority over the process.

6. Timeframes and Payouts

Processing times vary widely, but many states indicate typical ranges like:

  • Online, simple claims: 2–8 weeks
  • Mail or more complex claims: 8–16 weeks or longer

Payments are usually issued by check, sometimes ACH to a US account where allowed.

7. FAQ – Short Answers

Is this the same as “surplus funds” from tax sales?

Not exactly. Tax sale surplus is often handled at county level (e.g. Florida). Unclaimed property programs are state-level and include many other asset types.

Can foreigners claim unclaimed property?

In many cases yes, if they can prove their identity and connection to the account or address.

Do I need to pay a company to do this?

No. You can search and claim directly using official state websites. Third-party “finders” are optional.

Can I claim property that belongs to someone else?

Only if you are legally authorized (heir, executor, company officer, power of attorney) and can prove it.
