1. What is the Petco data breach settlement?

The Petco data breach settlement resolves legal claims after unauthorized parties accessed customer data stored within Petco systems. While financial information is typically not exposed in retail-related breaches, compromised personal data may still increase risks such as:

  • Identity theft attempts
  • Scam targeting using your personal information
  • Unauthorized account access
  • Fraudulent transactions linked indirectly to breached accounts

Without admitting wrongdoing, Petco agreed to fund a settlement providing cash reimbursement, time compensation, flat payments, and identity monitoring services.

Reminder: You must file a claim form to receive any benefits. If you do nothing, you will not receive payment.

2. Who can file a claim?

You may be eligible if:

  • You were a Petco customer during the settlement’s affected period
  • Your personal information was part of the breach
  • You received an official notice identifying you as a class member

Not sure? You can confirm by:

  • Checking the official settlement website
  • Using the Claim ID lookup tool
  • Contacting the settlement administrator

3. Compensation types – how payouts work

1) Documented Out-of-Pocket Losses

These reimburse expenses you paid because of the breach, including:

  • Replacement fees for identity documents
  • Costs to freeze or unfreeze credit
  • Bank fees or fraudulent charge liability
  • Credit monitoring purchased before the settlement offered it

Higher payouts require proper documentation.

2) Time Spent Compensation

Many data breach settlements pay for time spent dealing with breach consequences. Examples include:

  • Contacting credit bureaus or banks
  • Updating passwords and reviewing accounts
  • Resolving unauthorized activity

Time is compensated at a preset hourly rate up to a limit.

3) No-Proof Flat Cash Payment

If you lack documentation, you may still qualify for a flat payout intended for general inconvenience and monitoring obligations.

4) Identity Monitoring & Fraud Resolution

Many settlements include free monitoring services for several years, providing early alerts and identity theft recovery support.

4. What documents should you prepare?

To increase your payout, gather:

  • Receipts for expenses related to fraud prevention or recovery
  • Bank or credit card statements showing fraudulent activity
  • Emails confirming unauthorized account access
  • Invoices for replacement documents

Your documentation should clearly show:

  • Date of the expense or incident
  • Amount paid
  • Vendor or institution involved
  • How it relates to the breach
Good documentation significantly increases your chances of receiving the highest allowed payout.

5. Step-by-step guide: filing your Petco claim

Step 1 — Locate your Claim ID

  • Check your email for an official settlement notice
  • Search your physical mail for a postcard notice
  • Use the lookup tool on the settlement website

Step 2 — Choose your compensation type

Decide whether you want to claim:

  • Documented losses
  • Time spent
  • No-proof flat payment
  • Identity monitoring only

Step 3 — File online (recommended)

  • Go to the official settlement website
  • Click “Submit Claim”
  • Enter your Claim ID
  • Answer the questions truthfully
  • Upload documents if needed
  • Save your confirmation number

Step 4 — Paper filing (optional)

  • Download and print the claim form
  • Fill it out accurately
  • Attach copies of supporting documents
  • Mail it using a trackable service

6. Key deadlines

Deadlines typically include:

  • Claim submission deadline
  • Opt-out deadline
  • Objection deadline
  • Final approval hearing date

Missing the deadline usually disqualifies you from receiving settlement benefits.

7. What happens after you submit your claim?

After submission, the administrator:

  • Reviews your claim and documentation
  • Verifies eligibility
  • Approves or adjusts payments according to the rules

Payments are distributed after:

  • The court grants final approval
  • Any appeals are resolved
  • Claim audits are completed

Processing may take several months. Updates will be posted on the settlement website.

FAQ (2026)

Can I file a claim without proof?

Yes. Most settlements offer a flat, no-proof payment tier.

Can non-U.S. residents file?

Eligibility depends on the settlement’s class definition, but most settlements limit claims to affected customers within the relevant jurisdictions.
