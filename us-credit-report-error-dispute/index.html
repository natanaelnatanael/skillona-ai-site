US Credit Report Error Dispute – 2025 Guide

How to dispute errors on your US credit reports with Equifax, Experian and TransUnion: step-by-step process, what evidence to send and how to write effective dispute letters.

1. Why Credit Report Errors Matter

Incorrect negative information on your credit report can increase interest rates, cause loan denials and affect housing or job applications.

Common errors include:

  • Accounts that do not belong to you
  • Wrong balances or limits
  • Late payments reported by mistake
  • Outdated negative items that should have fallen off
  • Duplicate entries of the same debt

2. Step 1 – Get Your Credit Reports

You must see exactly what each bureau reports before disputing. In the US, there are official ways to get free or low-cost access to:

  • Equifax report
  • Experian report
  • TransUnion report

Save PDFs or screenshots and highlight the items you believe are wrong.

3. Step 2 – Identify and Classify Each Error

For each questionable item, note:

  • Which bureau reports it
  • Creditor name and account number (masked)
  • Type of error (not mine, wrong balance, wrong dates, etc.)
  • Why it is incorrect (with evidence)

This becomes the core of your dispute file.

4. Step 3 – Prepare Evidence

Useful evidence may include:

  • Bank statements
  • Payment confirmations
  • Letters or emails from creditors
  • Identity theft reports (if applicable)
  • Proof of address or employment for mixed-file cases
Tip

Attach only relevant pages and clearly mark where the evidence supports your claim. Reviewers have limited time.

5. Step 4 – File Disputes with Each Bureau

You can usually dispute online, by mail, or by phone. Written disputes (online or letter) are easier to track.

Key elements of a good dispute:

  • Your full name, address and date of birth
  • Report number (if provided)
  • Clear identification of each item being disputed
  • Short explanation why it is wrong
  • What you want them to do (correct, delete, update)
  • Copies (not originals) of your evidence
Sample short dispute paragraph

“I am disputing Account #XXXX with [Creditor]. This account does not belong to me. I have never had an account with this creditor. Please investigate and remove this account from my report.”

6. Investigation Timeline and Results

Credit bureaus generally have about 30 days to investigate and respond (longer in some cases if you provide new information later).

Possible outcomes:

  • Information is corrected
  • Information is deleted
  • No change (if creditor verifies as accurate)

You should receive updated reports or letters explaining the results.

7. If the Error Is Not Fixed

If you still believe the information is wrong, you can:

  • Dispute again with more detailed evidence
  • Contact the creditor directly and request correction
  • Add a short consumer statement to your report (if appropriate)
  • Consult a consumer law attorney if serious harm is involved
Important

Do not send vague, mass-dispute letters for everything – focus on genuinely inaccurate items. Abusive patterns can be ignored or treated as frivolous.

8. FAQ – Short Answers

Can disputing hurt my credit score?

The dispute itself usually does not hurt your score. If wrong negative items are removed, your score may improve.

How often can I dispute?

There is no strict limit, but repeating the same dispute without new information can be treated as frivolous.

Do I need to pay a “credit repair” company?

No. You can dispute directly for free. Some people choose services for convenience, but many just use templates and do it themselves.
