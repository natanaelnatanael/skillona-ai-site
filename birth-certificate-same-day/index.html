  • Parent or legal guardian (with ID)
  • Spouse, child, grandchild, sibling (with proof of relationship)
  • Legal representative (court order or power of attorney)

4. What to Bring – Universal Checklist

Primary ID (one): driver's license, state ID, passport, military ID
Secondary ID (if name changed): marriage certificate, divorce decree, court order
Proof of relationship (if not you): birth certificate of child, court order, power of attorney
Cash or card: most offices accept Visa/MC; some take only exact cash

9. FAQ – Everything Google Keeps Asking

How much does same-day birth certificate cost?

Walk-in: $15–$34 (state fee). VitalChek overnight: +$45. Courier: $79–$149.

Can I get it today if I was born in another state?

Yes – use VitalChek expedited or courier service – ships overnight to any US address.

What if I don’t have ID?

Bring two secondary documents: utility bill + Social Security card, or sworn statement from relative + their ID.

Can undocumented immigrants apply?

Yes – foreign passport + consular ID accepted in CA, TX, NY, IL, FL.

Can I get a copy same day?

Yes – certified copy is what you receive; it’s legally equivalent to original.

Can I get it for my newborn?

Hospital sends data to vital-records office within 5 days. Walk-in available after 10 days in most states.

What if the office is closed?

Use VitalChek weekend service – orders placed Sat/Sun before 2 PM ship Monday.

Can I track the shipment?

Yes – VitalChek and all couriers provide FedEx tracking automatically.

Can I pick it up for someone else?

Yes – bring notarized authorization letter + their ID copy + your ID.

Can I dispute a wrong birth certificate?

Yes – file amendment (different form) – same-day correction possible in some states (details in the PDF).
