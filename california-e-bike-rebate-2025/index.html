1. What Actually Happened to the California E-Bike Program?

California’s statewide E-Bike Incentive Project (run by CARB) offered income-qualified residents up to about $2 000 in vouchers – but by late 2025 the program was shut down and its funds redirected to car incentives.

That means: there is no active statewide voucher as of the last update. But you can still stack:

  • City / county e-bike rebates and micromobility grants
  • Utility and air-district incentives
  • Employer commuter benefits and health-plan perks
Bottom line for 2025

You probably won’t get a state voucher “button” anymore – but with 2–3 smaller programs together, the net cost of a mid-range e-bike can still be very close to zero.

2. Who Typically Qualifies in California?

Most current e-bike incentives in California look for:

  • California residency (any immigration status – ITIN or foreign passport is usually OK)
  • Income limits – often around 225–300 % of the federal poverty level
  • Living in a specific city, county or utility territory
  • Buying a qualifying Class 1–3 e-bike from an approved retailer

Some utility / employer programs have no income limit – they just require that you are a customer or employee.

5. Income Limits – How Programs Usually Work

Most low-income e-bike programs in California use a multiplier of the Federal Poverty Level (FPL), for example:

  • Standard applicants: up to 300 % FPL
  • Priority applicants (very low income): up to 225 % FPL

The exact dollar amounts change every year. Your kit includes a simple worksheet to check where your household income sits relative to the current FPL chart.

9. FAQ – Realistic Answers for 2025

Can I still get a statewide California e-bike voucher?

Not at the moment. The main statewide project has been discontinued. If it comes back, it will be announced on the official CARB site and will look more like a lottery than an instant “click and get money” system.

How long does it actually take to get money?

City / county programs: often a few weeks. Utility rebates: 2–8 weeks is common. Point-of-sale programs make the bike cheaper immediately, but the shop waits for the refund – not you.

Can I choose any e-bike?

It depends. Many programs allow any Class 1–3 bike above a minimum quality/price, bought from a participating or licensed retailer. Some exclude used bikes or online-only purchases.

Do I need a driver’s license?

Usually not. Most e-bike incentives are open to adults with a state ID or other accepted ID – no car license required.

Can undocumented immigrants apply?

Often yes, as long as you live in the service area and can show identity and income. Many programs accept ITINs and foreign passports.

Can I combine more than one rebate?

Sometimes. For example, a city rebate + employer stipend, or a utility credit + health-plan bonus. Read each program’s “stacking” rules carefully before you rely on it.

What if my application is denied?

Most public programs have an appeal or review option. The PDF kit includes a short “denial review” letter template you can adapt and send with extra documents.

Will these rebates be around forever?

No. They are funded year-to-year and can vanish or pause when budgets change. That’s why you apply as soon as you see an open window.
