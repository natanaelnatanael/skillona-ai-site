1. What Is a "Last Paycheck" and Why It Matters

The "last paycheck" (also called final pay) is the money your employer owes you after you stop working — including unpaid hours, accrued vacation (in most states), commissions, bonuses and expense reimbursements.

Federal law (FLSA) says you must be paid at least the federal minimum wage, but state laws decide when the check must arrive. Missing the deadline can trigger penalty pay (up to 30 days of extra wages) and wage-claim investigations.

Quick reality-check

1 in 4 workers never receives their final pay on time. Employers count on you giving up. This guide ensures you don't.

2. Who Can Use This Guide?

  • Employees who quit, resigned, retired
  • Workers who were laid off or fired
  • Undocumented workers (same rights under FLSA)
  • Foreign nationals (no SSN required to file wage claim)
  • Heirs of deceased employees

4. Step-by-Step: How to Get Your Last Paycheck Fast

  1. Calculate the exact amount owed (hours, OT, PTO, commissions).
  2. Text / email your manager (template below) – 80 % solved here.
  3. Send a formal demand letter (PDF template) citing the state statute.
  4. File a wage claim online (links in PDF) – free, no lawyer.
  5. Escalate to small-claims court if >$5 000 or claim rejected.
Pro tip

Most states let you add penalty wages to the claim. In CA that's 1 day of pay for every day late – often 2-3× the original check.

7. FAQ – Everything Google Keeps Asking

Can I file a wage claim without a Social Security Number?

Yes. Agencies accept ITIN or foreign passport. Payment can be wired internationally.

What if I was paid in cash or under the table?

You can still file. Bring any proof: schedules, photos, witness statements, text messages.

How long does a wage-claim investigation take?

California: 60-120 days. Texas: 90-150 days. NY: 45-90 days. Faster if employer cooperates.

Can my employer retaliate?

Illegal under federal and most state laws. Retaliation = separate penalty claim.

Can heirs file for a deceased worker?

Yes. Supply death certificate + small-estate affidavit or probate papers.

Is there a deadline to file?

2-3 years in most states (CA: 3 y, TX: 180 d, FL: 2 y). PDF contains full matrix.

Can I add vacation or commissions to the claim?

If accrued and promised – yes. Include calculations in the form.

Do I need a lawyer?

Not for agency claims. For court >$10 k or class-actions – recommended.

What if the company closed?

File anyway – state trust funds or federal WARN assets may pay.

Can I sue in small-claims court instead?

Yes, if the amount is within your state limit ($5 k-$15 k). Faster, no appeal.

Will this show up on a background check?

No. Wage claims are civil administrative matters, not criminal records.

Can undocumented workers file?

Yes. ICE policy prohibits using wage-claim data for immigration enforcement.

Can I still file if I signed a release?

If the release is in bad faith or violates public policy – usually void.

What if I was an independent contractor?

If mis-classified, file SS-8 with IRS first, then wage claim.

Can I claim emotional distress?

Only in court, not in agency claim. Needs separate civil suit.

Can I file against a former manager personally?

Only if they acted as employer (controlled payroll, hiring, firing).

How do I enforce the agency decision?

Convert it to a court judgment – then bank levy or lien.

Can I settle after filing?

Yes – agencies encourage settlement conferences. Get it in writing.

What if I forgot to file for 5 years?

Statute probably expired, but you can still send a demand letter – sometimes works.

Can I file while living abroad?

Yes – use ITIN, foreign bank wire, email for hearings (Zoom allowed post-COVID).

Can I add interest to the claim?

Many states auto-add statutory interest (e.g., NY 6 %, CA 10 %).

Will the employer know I filed?

Yes – they receive a copy and must respond. That's the point.

Can I file anonymously?

No – employer needs notice. Retaliation protections apply.

What if I don't know the exact amount?

Estimate – agency will calculate during audit. Provide pay stubs if you have any.
