Florida Property Tax Appeal – 2025 Step-by-Step Guide

How to appeal your Florida property tax assessment if you believe the value is too high. Deadlines, evidence, forms and short scripts you can use with the Property Appraiser and Value Adjustment Board (VAB).

1. When Does a Property Tax Appeal Make Sense?

An appeal is most useful when the assessed value is clearly higher than market value or when the county made a factual mistake about your property.

Good reasons to appeal include:

  • Assessed value is significantly above recent comparable sales
  • Square footage, condition or features are wrong in county records
  • The property has unusual damage or issues not reflected in the value
  • Homestead or other exemptions were not applied
Bad reasons

“Taxes are too high in general” is not an appeal argument. You must show why the assessed value for your property is incorrect.

2. Timeline – Key Dates in Florida

Exact dates vary by county, but the general pattern is:

  • Notice of Proposed Property Taxes (TRIM notice) mailed around August
  • Informal discussion period with Property Appraiser shortly after notice
  • Formal appeal to the Value Adjustment Board (VAB) within a strict deadline (often 25 days after TRIM)

Missing the VAB filing deadline can mean you have to wait until the next year.

3. Step 1 – Read Your TRIM Notice Carefully

On the TRIM notice, identify:

  • Assessed value vs. market value
  • Exemptions (homestead, etc.)
  • Estimated tax amounts by taxing authority
  • Instructions and deadline for filing a petition

Compare the assessed value to what you believe the property would realistically sell for today.

4. Step 2 – Collect Evidence

Useful evidence includes:

  • Recent comparable sales (same area, similar size and condition)
  • Photos of defects or needed repairs
  • Inspection reports
  • Appraisal reports, if available
  • Rent roll and expenses for income properties (to support an income approach)
Tip

Focus on the value on January 1 of the tax year, not today’s value later in the year.

5. Step 3 – Informal Contact with the Property Appraiser

Before filing a formal petition, many counties encourage an informal review.

Short script for phone or email:

Sample script

“Hello, I received my TRIM notice for parcel [number]. I believe the assessed value is too high compared to similar properties. I have some sales data and photos. Could we review the assessment together before I file a VAB petition?”

Sometimes values are adjusted at this stage without a formal hearing.

6. Step 4 – Filing a Formal Petition (VAB)

If informal review does not solve the issue, file a petition with the Value Adjustment Board. Typically you will:

  • Use the county’s online system or a paper form (often Form DR-486)
  • Pay a small filing fee (varies by county)
  • Indicate your reason for appeal (market value, classification, exemption, etc.)

Keep proof of submission and payment.

7. Step 5 – Preparing for the Hearing

For the VAB hearing, prepare:

  • Summary of your argument (1–2 pages)
  • Comparable sales with addresses, dates, and prices
  • Photos and reports showing condition issues
  • Any appraisals or professional opinions

Be ready to explain calmly why your proposed value is more accurate than the county’s value.

8. FAQ – Short Answers

Can foreign owners appeal property taxes?

Yes. Property tax appeals are based on the property and its value, not your citizenship or residency.

Does appealing reduce my tax rate?

No. You are appealing the assessed value, not the tax rate. Lower value = lower taxes.

Do I need a lawyer or tax representative?

Many owners handle simple appeals themselves. For large portfolios or complex cases, some hire property tax consultants or attorneys on a fee or contingency basis.

Will appealing upset the county?

No. Appeals are normal and part of the process. Just present facts respectfully.
