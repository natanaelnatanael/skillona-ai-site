Florida Property Tax Appeal – 2025 Step-by-Step Guide

1. When Does a Florida Property Tax Appeal Make Sense?

An appeal can be worth exploring when the assessed value appears out of line with the property and the local market, or when there may be a factual issue in the county records.

Examples of reasons owners often review or question their assessment include:

  • Assessed value seems significantly above recent comparable sales in the neighborhood.
  • Square footage, condition or key features appear inaccurate in the property record card.
  • The property has meaningful damage or unique issues not reflected in the value.
  • Certain exemptions (for example, homestead or other listed exemptions) may not have been applied.
Less effective arguments

General concerns like “taxes are too high” across the area usually do not address the core question, which is whether the assessed value for your specific parcel is a reasonable estimate of value under Florida law for that tax year.

2. Timeline – Typical Florida Property Tax Appeal Dates

Exact dates and procedures are set at the county level, but many Florida counties follow a similar pattern. Always check your TRIM notice and county website for current information.

  • TRIM notice mailed: often around August, with proposed assessments and rates.
  • Informal review window: a period shortly after the notice when you can contact the Property Appraiser\u2019s office.
  • Formal VAB petition deadline: a strict deadline (often around 25 days after the TRIM notice date) to file a petition with the Value Adjustment Board.

If the formal petition deadline is missed, options can be much more limited and you may have to wait for the next assessment cycle unless your county allows late-file procedures in specific circumstances.

3. Step 1 – Read Your TRIM Notice Carefully

On the TRIM notice, identify key elements such as:

  • Assessed value vs. market/just value.
  • Exemptions that are listed (for example, homestead, veteran, senior).
  • Estimated tax amounts by each taxing authority.
  • Instructions and deadline for filing a petition with the VAB.

Compare the assessed value to a realistic estimate of what the property could have sold for around the relevant valuation date (typically January 1 of the tax year).

4. Step 2 – Collect Supporting Evidence

Examples of evidence owners often gather include:

  • Recent comparable sales (similar size, age, condition and location).
  • Photos of defects, deferred maintenance or unusual issues.
  • Inspection reports or contractor estimates for significant repairs.
  • Appraisal reports, if you have them.
  • For income properties, rent rolls and operating expenses that support an income-based value.
Valuation date reminder

Florida property tax assessments generally look at value as of a specific valuation date (commonly January 1 of the tax year). Evidence closest to that date often carries more weight than later changes.

5. Step 3 – Informal Contact with the Property Appraiser

Before filing a formal petition, many counties encourage an informal review with the Property Appraiser\u2019s office. This can sometimes resolve questions without a hearing.

Example script for phone or email (adapt as needed):

Sample owner script

“Hello, I received my TRIM notice for parcel [number]. I believe the assessed value may be high compared to similar properties. I have some sales data and photos I would like to share. Could we review the assessment together before I decide whether to file a Value Adjustment Board petition?”

The office may explain how they arrived at the value and, in some cases, may adjust the assessment based on new information.

6. Step 4 – Filing a Formal Petition with the VAB

If the informal review does not resolve your concern, the next step is to file a petition with the Value Adjustment Board within the stated deadline.

Typical elements (check your county instructions):

  • Filing electronically through the county portal or submitting a paper petition (often based on Form DR-486 or local equivalent).
  • Paying a filing fee (amount and any waivers vary by county).
  • Indicating the type of issue (for example: value, classification, exemption).

Keep copies of the petition, any confirmations and proof of payment for your records.

7. Step 5 – Preparing for the VAB Hearing

For a VAB hearing, it usually helps to organise your materials clearly for whoever will review them:

  • A short written summary (1–2 pages) of your position and your suggested value.
  • A list of comparable sales with addresses, sale dates and prices, and brief notes on why they are comparable.
  • Photos, reports or estimates showing condition issues that impact value.
  • Any appraisal reports or professional opinions you want considered.

During the hearing, staying concise, factual and focused on valuation can make it easier for the board or special magistrate to follow your argument.

8. FAQ – Florida Property Tax Appeal Basics

Can foreign owners appeal property taxes?

In many Florida counties, the appeal process is open to the recorded owner or their authorised representative, regardless of residency. The focus is on the property and assessment. Check your county\u2019s rules and consider written authorisation if someone else will represent you.

Does appealing reduce my tax rate?

The appeal process typically concerns the value placed on your property. Tax rates are set separately by taxing authorities. A successful appeal that lowers the assessed value can reduce the tax amount that results from applying those rates.

Do I need a lawyer or tax representative?

Many owners handle straightforward appeals themselves. For higher-value properties, complex income calculations or legal questions, some owners choose to work with property tax consultants or attorneys who work with Florida property tax law on a regular basis.

Will appealing upset the county?

Counties process a significant number of questions and petitions each year, and the appeal process is part of Florida\u2019s property tax system. Approaching the process respectfully and with documentation usually keeps the interaction professional.
