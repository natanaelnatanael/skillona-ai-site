1. Why Background-Check Errors Matter

1 in 5 reports contains a material error (FTC 2023). Wrong felony, eviction, or DOB can cost you a job, apartment, loan, insurance or license. The Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) gives you the right to dispute and force a free correction within 30 days.

Bottom line

You do not need a lawyer or a paid "credit-repair" company. This page shows the exact buttons, addresses and sentences to use.

2. Who Can Dispute?

  • Any consumer who obtained a copy of their report (employer/landlord must give you one)
  • Foreign nationals (ITIN or passport is accepted)
  • Heirs of deceased consumers (death certificate + small-estate affidavit)
  • Minors (parent / guardian signs)
  • Incarcerated persons (mail-in accepted)

3. Four-Step Dispute Framework (Works for All CRAs)

  1. Pull your own report (free links below) and highlight every error.
  2. Gather proof (court dismissal, identity theft police report, pay stubs, lease).
  3. Submit dispute (online fastest, certified mail strongest evidence).
  4. Track 30-day clock – if not corrected, file FCRA lawsuit or CFPB complaint.

6. Proof Checklist – What to Attach

Identity: government photo ID + utility bill (foreign passport accepted)
Police/Identity-Theft: FTC identity-theft report (identitytheft.gov) + local police report
Court: certified dismissal, expungement order, disposition sheet
Civil: satisfaction of judgment, release of lien, lease termination letter
Employment: W-2, pay stubs, corrected HR letter
Education: diploma, transcript, registrar letter
Banking: paid-in-full letter, account closure letter
Death: death certificate + small-estate affidavit or probate order

9. What Happens After You Submit?

  1. CRA has 30 calendar days to complete reinvestigation (21 for employment reports).
  2. They forward your dispute to the furnisher (court, bank, landlord, employer).
  3. Furnisher must certify accuracy or delete the item.
  4. CRA sends you free revised report and notice of results.
  5. If corrected, you can request CRA to send the corrected report to anyone who received the erroneous one in the last 6 months (free).
If they "verify" the error as accurate

You have the right to add a 100-word consumer statement, but better: file a second-level dispute with new evidence or go straight to FCRA lawsuit (15 U.S.C. § 1681n) for damages.

10. Second-Level Dispute & Furnisher Direct Dispute

If the CRA "verifies" the incorrect data, send a direct dispute to the furnisher (15 U.S.C. § 1681s-2(a)(8)) using the same evidence + CRA response. Furnisher has 30 days to respond. Silence = deletion.

Furnisher direct-dispute addresses are inside the PDF (400+ companies).

11. Sue for FCRA Damages – When & How

  • Negligent violation: actual damages + costs
  • Willful violation: statutory damages $100 – $1 000 per item + punitive + attorney fees
  • Time-limit: 2 years from discovery, 5 years from violation
  • Venue: federal district court or state court
  • Small-claims shortcut: ≤$10 k in most states – no lawyer, 30-day trial

Template complaint + small-claims packet inside the PDF.

12. CFPB & FTC Complaints – Free Nuclear Option

File online at consumerfinance.gov/complaint (CRA or bank) and reportfraud.ftc.gov (identity theft). Attach dispute proof. Agencies usually respond within 15 days and force deletion in many cases.

13. Special Scenarios

Foreign Nationals / No SSN

Use ITIN or passport number. All CRAs accept international address for results.

Heirs of Deceased Consumer

Attach death certificate + small-estate affidavit or court appointment. Dispute proceeds same way.

Military – Active-Duty Alert

Place alert via SCRA portal – CRAs must verify identity before reporting.

Expunged / Sealed Records

Attach certified court order. If county still shows on website, contact court clerk to update NCIC.

Identity Theft

File FTC identity-theft report (identitytheft.gov) + local police. CRAs must block fraudulent items within 4 days.

14. FAQ – Everything People Also Ask

How long does a background-check dispute take?

CRA: 30 days (21 for employment). Furnisher direct: 30 days. CFPB complaint: around 15 days for first response.

Can I dispute online or must I mail?

Online is fastest, but certified mail gives you better evidence if you later sue.

What if the error reappears later?

That's "re-insertion". CRA must notify you within 5 days. If they don't, you can sue again under FCRA.

Do I need to pay a credit-repair company?

No. Everything they do you can do yourself for free using this process.

Can I dispute a DUI that was dismissed?

Yes. Attach the certified dismissal order. CRA must show it as dismissed or remove it.

Can employers see my dispute?

No. They receive only the updated report, not your dispute letters.

Can I dispute ChexSystems or Innovis?

Yes. Same FCRA rules apply; addresses are in the PDF kit.

Can I dispute a report I never received?

First request your free file, then dispute the errors you find.
