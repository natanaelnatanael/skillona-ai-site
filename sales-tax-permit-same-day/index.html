Why this matters

1. Why same-day sales tax registration is a big deal

Amazon, Shopify, Etsy, craft fairs and most marketplaces expect you to have a sales tax permit before your first taxable sale. States can charge penalties for operating without one: in some places it starts around $50 per day or $100 per month, plus interest.

The good news: in 2025, most states issue your permit number instantly online. You just need the correct dashboard, a clean EIN checklist and a few numbers ready.

Who needs a permit

2. Who actually needs a sales tax permit in 2025?

You generally need a permit if you have nexus in a state and sell taxable goods or services. Common situations:

  • E-commerce stores ‚Äì Amazon FBA, Shopify, WooCommerce, Etsy, eBay, Walmart.
  • Dropshipping ‚Äì Shopify + CJ, Spocket, Printful, etc.
  • Offline sales ‚Äì craft fairs, pop-up shops, farmers markets, mall kiosks.
  • Digital products ‚Äì courses, PDFs, software and apps in states that tax them.
  • Service providers in some SSUTA states where services are taxable.
  • Foreign sellers shipping to US customers under marketplace facilitator rules.

Process

4. Universal same-day process (works in every portal)

Most dashboards ask the same core questions. You can reuse the exact answers state by state.

  1. Get an EIN (if you do not already have one) at irs.gov.
  2. Choose the correct state portal from the table or PDF kit and create an account.
  3. Complete registration ‚Äì legal name, trade name, address, NAICS code, ownership type, first sales date and estimated monthly sales.
  4. Download your permit or confirmation letter as soon as it appears. Save it as PDF, upload it into your marketplace dashboards and keep a copy in your records.

EIN setup

5. EIN checklist ‚Äì do this before opening any state portal

An EIN is the federal tax ID that almost all states ask for when you register. Getting one is free and usually instant online.

  • Legal business name (or your own name if you are a sole proprietor).
  • SSN or ITIN (foreign sellers can usually rely on EIN only once created).
  • Business mailing address ‚Äì can be home, office or virtual mailbox.
  • NAICS code for your main activity.
  • Rough estimate of your first-year revenue.
i
Apply at irs.gov only ‚Äì not through paid intermediaries. The IRS application is free and gives you an EIN letter you can reuse with banks, payment processors and state agencies.
Foreign sellers

6. Foreign sellers (no SSN) ‚Äì what still works in 2025

Selling from outside the US does not exempt you from state tax rules. Fortunately, many states accept:

  • An EIN obtained as a foreign entity with no SSN.
  • A virtual US mailing address as your business address.
  • Marketplace facilitator collection when you only sell through large platforms.

Where a Social Security Number is requested, portals often allow you to choose ‚Äúforeign‚Äù or enter only EIN ‚Äì the PDF kit lists states where this works smoothly and those where you may need to mail a paper form instead.

Marketplace facilitators

7. Marketplace facilitator exception ‚Äì when you don‚Äôt need a permit

If you sell only through marketplace facilitators such as Amazon, Etsy, Walmart, eBay or some Shopify-powered marketplaces, these platforms are usually required to collect and remit sales tax on your behalf.

In many states that means you do not need your own permit as long as:

  • You have no separate website taking orders in that state.
  • You do not invoice customers directly or run wholesale operations.
  • You stay below any economic nexus thresholds for your direct sales.
!
The moment you start selling via your own website, funnels, invoicing or in-person events, the marketplace shield no longer covers those sales. That is when a state permit becomes mandatory.
Offline & backup routes

8. When online portals fail ‚Äì paper and walk-in options

Occasionally dashboards are down or identity checks fail. In that case, states almost always have a backup route:

  • Walk-in registration at regional tax offices (common in CA, TX, FL) with your paper form, ID and EIN letter.
  • Mailed forms that you can send by courier with tracking. Processing takes longer but is still straightforward.
  • Phone support lines that will confirm if you are allowed to start selling while approval is pending.

Compliance tools

9. Filing, automation and practical FAQs

How long does it take to get a sales tax permit?
Online approval is often instant in around 40 states. Paper applications and mailed forms usually take 3‚Äì10 business days. Some states also offer same-day walk-in registration at district offices.
Is there a fee?
Many states charge no registration fee at all. A few charge a small application or deposit, such as Florida (~$5) or Wisconsin and New Jersey with higher deposits that can be refundable when you close the account in good standing.
Do I need a separate permit for each state?
Yes. Nexus in a state generally means a separate registration. Streamlined Sales Tax (SSUTA) states let you register in a more coordinated way, but it is still one account per state.
Can I sell without a permit?
Operating without a required permit is illegal. States can assess back tax, penalties, interest and in some cases classify it as a misdemeanor. It is usually easier and cheaper to register properly from day one.
Can foreign sellers get a permit?
Yes. Most states allow foreign entities to register using an EIN and a mailing address. Some require additional documentation or paper forms, which is covered in the PDF walkthrough.
Do I need a permit for digital products?
Digital products are taxable in roughly half the states. Where they are taxable, you use the same permit application as for physical goods, just with a different product type or NAICS code.
Can I automate filings with TaxJar or similar tools?
Yes. Tools such as TaxJar, Avalara and others can connect to your store and marketplaces, calculate the correct tax and file returns automatically. They do not replace registration, but they reduce manual work once your permits are active.
