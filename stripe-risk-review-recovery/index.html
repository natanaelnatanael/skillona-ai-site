1. Why Stripe Flags or Shuts Down Accounts

Common triggers include:

  • High chargeback or dispute rates
  • Sudden spikes in volume or ticket size
  • Business models in restricted or higher-risk categories
  • Mismatched or incomplete KYC information
  • Complaints, refunds and negative user patterns

Stripe is primarily protecting against fraud, money laundering, regulatory risk and excessive chargebacks.

2. Types of Stripe Actions

  • Additional information request: documents needed, account still processing
  • Payout delay / reserve: funds held for longer before payout
  • Account “under review”: risk team is analyzing your business
  • Account closure: processing disabled; remaining funds held for a period

The email from Stripe usually states clearly which case you are in. Read it carefully before replying.

3. First Response: Do This Immediately

  • Read the full email and dashboard notifications from Stripe
  • Make a list of all documents they request
  • Pause any risky or unclear offers while under review
  • Prepare a concise explanation of your business

Responding fast, completely and professionally improves your chances more than arguing.

4. Documents Stripe Often Asks For

  • Government ID for owners/directors
  • Business registration documents (LLC, company, etc.)
  • Proof of address and website
  • Product or service description (landing pages, terms, refund policy)
  • Invoices or contracts with customers
  • Evidence of delivery or access (for digital products)
Tip

Make it easy for the risk team to understand what you sell, who your customers are, how you deliver and how you handle refunds. Clarity reduces perceived risk.

5. How to Write a Strong Explanation

Your explanation should:

  • Describe your business in one or two sentences
  • Explain who your typical customers are
  • Explain how you deliver the product or service
  • Highlight your refund and support policies
  • Mention any steps you take to avoid disputes or fraud

Example (for digital products):

Sample explanation

“We sell digital training materials and templates to small business owners. Customers buy via our website and receive instant access by email and in their account. We provide email support and a 14-day refund policy. We monitor for unusual activity and refund proactively if customers report issues to avoid disputes.”

6. If Stripe Is Holding or Delaying Payouts

Stripe may introduce rolling reserves or longer payout schedules if they consider your business higher risk but still acceptable.

In that case:

  • Ask what changes would reduce the reserve or delay (e.g. lower chargebacks, more history)
  • Adjust your cash flow planning to account for slower payouts
  • Consider splitting volume across multiple processors (if allowed by terms)

7. If Stripe Decides to Close the Account

Closure emails are usually firm. You can still reply and ask if there is any possibility of reconsideration, but they often stick to the decision.

Funds may be held for a period (for example 90 days) to cover potential chargebacks. After that, remaining balance is typically paid out.

Important

Do not attempt to open multiple new Stripe accounts under different names or entities solely to bypass a closure. This can create further compliance issues.

8. FAQ – Short Answers

Can I save my Stripe account once it is “under review”?

Sometimes yes. Clear documents and a strong explanation can convince Stripe your business is acceptable. Results vary by case.

Why was I approved at first and then flagged later?

Many reviews are triggered by real-time transaction patterns, not just your initial application.

Can I ask Stripe exactly what is wrong?

You can ask, but they may not share full internal risk criteria. Focus on what you can control: clarity, documentation and lower dispute rates.
