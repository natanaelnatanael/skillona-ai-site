US Utility Deposit Refund Guide – 2025

How to get your security deposits back from electric, gas, water and internet providers when you move, switch providers or close an account. Simple checklist and scripts.

1. What Is a Utility Deposit and When Is It Refundable?

A utility deposit is money a provider holds as security when you start service (electricity, gas, water, internet, cable, etc.).

It is usually refundable when:

  • You close the account and final bill is paid
  • Or you keep a good payment history for a period (for example 12 months)
Key point

Many people forget about old deposits when they move. Providers are not always proactive about reminding you.

2. Step 1 – List All Current and Past Providers

Make a simple list of:

  • Electric, gas, water, trash, internet, cable providers
  • Addresses and dates where you used each provider
  • Account numbers if you still have them

This helps you systematically check for deposits at each provider instead of guessing.

3. Step 2 – Check Contracts, Old Emails and Bills

Look for lines like “security deposit” or “deposit held” on:

  • Welcome letters
  • First invoice
  • Account summaries in the online portal

If you don’t see it clearly, you can still ask the provider whether a deposit was ever charged.

4. Step 3 – Contact Provider and Ask About Deposit Status

Short phone or chat script:

Sample script

“Hello, my name is [Name]. I had service at [address] with account number [if known]. I want to confirm whether there was a security deposit on this account and if it has been refunded or applied to my bill.”

Write down what the agent says for each provider.

5. Step 4 – Closing an Account and Requesting Refund

When you move or switch providers, ask explicitly what happens to your deposit:

  • Will it be applied to the final bill?
  • Will any remaining amount be refunded by check or bank transfer?
  • What is the expected timeline (for example 4–8 weeks)?

Confirm your forwarding address or preferred refund method.

6. If the Deposit Wasn’t Refunded

If you suspect a deposit was never returned:

  • Ask for a statement showing how it was used or refunded
  • Provide proof of your identity and old address
  • Escalate to a supervisor if the answer is unclear
Important

Some old deposits may end up as unclaimed property with the state if the provider can’t reach you. In that case, also search state unclaimed property sites.

7. FAQ – Short Answers

How long does a provider have to return my deposit?

Time limits depend on state rules and your contract. It’s often within a few billing cycles after account closure.

Can deposits be kept if I owe money?

Yes. Providers commonly apply deposits to unpaid balances first and return only what is left.

What if the company no longer exists?

In some cases, old deposits may be transferred to another company or to state unclaimed property programs.
