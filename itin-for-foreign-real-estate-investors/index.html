Basics

1. What is an ITIN and why investors need it

An ITIN (Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) is a tax ID issued by the IRS to people who are not eligible for a Social Security number but still need to file a U.S. tax return or be reported on one.

As a foreign real estate investor, an ITIN is often needed to:

  • Report rental income and expenses from U.S. properties.
  • Claim deductions and avoid default 30% withholding on gross rent.
  • Be listed as a partner or member in a U.S. LLC or partnership.
  • Handle certain FIRPTA (sale) withholding refunds.
  • Open some U.S. bank/broker accounts that require a tax ID.
!
An ITIN is for tax purposes only. It does not give you work authorization, Social Security benefits, or immigration status in the U.S.

2. When you qualify for an ITIN as an investor

The IRS requires a tax reason to issue an ITIN. For real estate investors, common qualifying reasons are:

  • You are a non-resident alien who owns U.S. rental property and needs to file Form 1040-NR.
  • You are a member of an LLC or partnership that files a U.S. return and reports income allocated to you.
  • You are due a FIRPTA refund after selling U.S. real estate and need an ITIN to claim it.

In practice, you usually apply for an ITIN at the same time as your first U.S. tax return or together with specific withholding forms.

Application

3. Core application: IRS Form W-7

The ITIN application is made on Form W-7. Key parts for investors:

  • Reason you are submitting ‚Äì most often box ‚Äúa‚Äù or ‚Äúb‚Äù with explanation.
  • Your full legal name, foreign address and U.S. mailing address (if any).
  • Country of citizenship, birth information and identification documents.
  • Signature and date (must match your supporting ID).

Supporting documents you typically need

  • Passport (often enough by itself), or
  • National ID + birth certificate (if not using a passport).
  • Originals or certified copies by the issuing agency ‚Äì regular notarised copies are usually not accepted.

Investor ITIN starter checklist

  • Completed Form W-7 (signed).
  • Original passport or certified copy from issuing authority.
  • Draft or final Form 1040-NR showing rental income, or other tax form.
  • U.S. property details (address, closing statement, lease, etc.).

4. 3 main ways to submit an ITIN application

You generally have three main routes:

4.1. By mail directly to the IRS

  • You send Form W-7 + tax return + original ID directly to the IRS.
  • Risk: your passport is physically in the mail for several weeks.
  • Lower cost but slower and more stressful if you travel often.

4.2. Through a Certifying Acceptance Agent (CAA)

A CAA is authorised to verify your passport and send certified copies to the IRS so you do not have to mail the original.

  • Often available in many countries, not only in the U.S.
  • They charge a fee but dramatically reduce risk of ID loss.
  • Useful if you are an active investor with travel plans.

4.3. At selected IRS offices (if you are in the U.S.)

  • Some IRS Taxpayer Assistance Centers can review documents.
  • Usually by appointment only; locations are limited.
‚úì
For most foreign investors, using a CAA plus a tax professional who understands non-resident returns is the most practical path: less risk, fewer rejections, faster result.
Timing & renewal

5. How long does ITIN approval take?

Processing times can vary, but 6‚Äì12 weeks is a common range. At busy times of year, it can take longer.

The IRS will send your ITIN assignment notice by mail to the address you provided on Form W-7. Keep this letter safely ‚Äì banks and partners may ask to see it later.

6. ITIN expiration and renewal for investors

ITINs do not last forever. They can expire if:

  • You have not used the ITIN on a U.S. tax return for several years, or
  • Your ITIN falls into certain number ranges that the IRS has phased out.

If your ITIN expires, you must renew it by filing an updated W-7 (often without resubmitting a full tax return if you are only renewing).

Renewal quick steps

  • Check if the IRS notice indicates your ITIN is expiring.
  • Prepare a new Form W-7 with ‚Äúrenewal‚Äù box checked.
  • Attach updated identification documents as required.
  • Submit via mail, CAA or IRS office, similar to a new application.
Real estate context

7. ITIN and U.S. rental income

Without an ITIN and proper tax election, your U.S. rental income may be treated as FDAP income with a flat 30% withholding on gross rent, with no deduction for expenses.

By obtaining an ITIN and filing Form 1040-NR with a ‚Äúnet basis‚Äù election, you can usually:

  • Deduct mortgage interest, property taxes, repairs, management.
  • Depreciate the building over the allowed schedule.
  • Often pay tax only on the real net profit instead of gross rent.

8. ITIN and selling U.S. property (FIRPTA)

When a non-resident sells U.S. real estate, the buyer or closing agent often withholds 15% of the sale price under FIRPTA. If the withheld amount is more than your actual tax, you need an ITIN to:

  • File a return showing the real gain after expenses and basis.
  • Request a refund of excess FIRPTA withholding.

In some cases, you can apply for a FIRPTA withholding certificate to reduce withholding before closing ‚Äì this also typically requires an ITIN or coordinated application.
Mistakes to avoid

9. Common ITIN mistakes for foreign investors

  • Sending a regular photocopy of your passport instead of an original or certified copy from the issuing authority.
  • Not including a tax return or qualifying document when the category requires it.
  • Using the wrong mailing address and then never receiving the ITIN letter.
  • Letting the ITIN expire and then discovering it right before a property sale or refinancing.
  • Mixing up SSN and ITIN fields on bank, lender or IRS forms.

10. Quick FAQs

Can an LLC itself get an ITIN?
No. ITINs are for individuals only. LLCs and companies get an EIN. As a foreign member, you personally may still need an ITIN to be reported on the LLC‚Äôs return.
Can I invest in U.S. property without an ITIN?
In many cases you can close on a property using a passport and foreign address, especially through an LLC. But you will still need an ITIN later for tax filings and to avoid unfavourable default withholding.
Does ITIN status affect my immigration situation?
No. The ITIN is strictly a tax identifier. It does not grant or remove any immigration rights or visa status.
