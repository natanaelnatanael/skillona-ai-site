Basics

1. What is an ITIN and why investors need it

An ITIN (Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) is a tax ID issued by the IRS to people who are not eligible for a Social Security number but still need to be identified on a U.S. tax return or information form.

As a foreign real estate investor, an ITIN is often needed to:

  • Report rental income and expenses from U.S. properties on Form 1040-NR.
  • Claim deductions and avoid default 30% withholding on gross rent.
  • Be listed as a partner or member in a U.S. LLC or partnership.
  • Handle FIRPTA withholding when you sell U.S. property.
  • Open some U.S. bank/broker accounts that require a tax ID.
!
An ITIN is for tax purposes only. It does not give you work authorization, Social Security benefits, or any immigration status in the United States.

2. When you qualify for an ITIN as a foreign investor

The IRS requires a tax reason to issue an ITIN. For real estate investors, common qualifying reasons are:

  • You are a non-resident alien who owns U.S. rental property and needs to file Form 1040-NR.
  • You are a member of an LLC or partnership that files a U.S. return and reports income allocated to you.
  • You are due a FIRPTA refund after selling U.S. real estate and need an ITIN to claim the refund.

In practice, you usually apply for an ITIN at the same time as your first U.S. tax return or together with specific withholding or treaty forms prepared by your advisor.

Investor ITIN ‚Äì quick self-check

  • You already bought or are under contract to buy U.S. property.
  • You expect to receive rent or sale proceeds in your personal name.
  • You will be a member of a pass-through entity (LLC / partnership).
  • You will need to file Form 1040-NR or claim a FIRPTA refund.

Application paths

4. Three main ways to submit an ITIN application

You generally have three main routes as a foreign investor:

4.1. By mail directly to the IRS

  • You send Form W-7 + tax return + original ID directly to the IRS address listed in the instructions.
  • Risk: your passport is physically in the mail for several weeks.
  • Lower cost but slower and more stressful if you travel often.

4.2. Through a Certifying Acceptance Agent (CAA)

A CAA is authorised to verify your passport and send certified copies to the IRS so you do not have to mail the original document.

  • Many CAAs work fully online or via video call, in multiple countries.
  • They charge a fee but reduce risk of document loss and rejection.
  • Best option if you want to keep your passport with you.

4.3. At selected IRS offices (if you are in the U.S.)

  • Some IRS Taxpayer Assistance Centers can review your documents in person.
  • Usually by appointment only; locations are limited.
‚úì
For most foreign investors, using a CAA + non-resident tax professional is the most practical path: less ID risk, cleaner paperwork, fewer rejections.
Timing & renewal

5. How long ITIN approval takes

Processing times vary, but 6‚Äì12 weeks is typical. During peak season it can take longer. The IRS will send your ITIN assignment notice to the mailing address on Form W-7.

Keep that letter safe ‚Äì banks, lenders and partners may ask to see it when onboarding you as an investor.

6. ITIN expiration and renewal for investors

ITINs can expire if you do not use them on a U.S. tax return for several years or if they fall into blocks that the IRS phases out.

To renew, you file Form W-7 again with the renewal box checked. In many cases you can renew without attaching a full tax return if the IRS instructions allow that for your situation.

Renewal quick steps

  • Check if your ITIN is listed as expiring in IRS notices.
  • Prepare a new Form W-7 and tick the ‚Äúrenewal‚Äù option.
  • Attach updated identification documents if requested.
  • Submit via mail, CAA or IRS office, same as a new application.
Real estate context

7. ITIN and U.S. rental income

Without an ITIN and the correct tax election, your U.S. rental income may be treated as FDAP income with a flat 30% withholding on gross rent, with no deduction for expenses.

By obtaining an ITIN and filing Form 1040-NR with a ‚Äúnet basis‚Äù election, you can usually:

  • Deduct mortgage interest, property taxes, repairs and management fees.
  • Depreciate the building over the allowed schedule.
  • Often pay tax only on the real net profit instead of gross rent.

8. ITIN and selling U.S. property (FIRPTA)

When a non-resident sells U.S. real estate, the buyer or closing agent often withholds 15% of the sale price under FIRPTA. If the withheld amount is more than your actual tax, you need an ITIN to:

  • File a tax return showing the real gain after expenses and basis.
  • Request a refund of excess FIRPTA withholding.

In some cases, you can apply for a FIRPTA withholding certificate to reduce withholding before closing ‚Äì this usually requires coordination between your closing agent, tax advisor and ITIN application.
Mistakes

9. Common ITIN mistakes for foreign investors

  • Sending a regular photocopy of your passport instead of an original or a certified copy from the issuing authority.
  • Not including a tax return or qualifying document when your reason category requires it.
  • Using an unreliable mailing address and then never receiving the ITIN letter.
  • Letting the ITIN expire and discovering it right before a sale, refinance or capital raise.
  • Mixing up SSN and ITIN fields on bank, lender or IRS forms and causing extra checks or holds.

10. Quick FAQs

Can an LLC itself get an ITIN?
No. ITINs are for individuals only. LLCs and companies get an EIN. As a foreign member, you personally may still need an ITIN to be reported on the LLC‚Äôs U.S. tax return.
Can I invest in U.S. property without an ITIN?
In many cases you can close on a property using a passport and foreign address, especially through an LLC. But you will still need an ITIN later for tax filings, FIRPTA refunds and banking.
Does ITIN status affect my immigration situation?
No. The ITIN is strictly a tax identifier. It does not grant, extend or remove any immigration rights or visa status.
