1. What WHO changed in 2026

The new WHO guidance tightens global GLP-1 safety protocols due to rapid worldwide use. Key updates:

  • Mandatory baseline evaluation before prescribing.
  • More aggressive monitoring of pancreatitis & gallbladder signals.
  • Strict rules for when to taper instead of stopping abruptly.
  • Clear criteria limiting cosmetic/off-label “slimming only” use.

2. New safety monitoring requirements

WHO requires patients to track these “early-danger indicators” weekly:

  • Persistent upper-abdominal pain radiating to the back.
  • Severe nausea/vomiting unrelated to dose changes.
  • Distension, bloating, or painful fullness.
  • Upper-right abdominal pain (possible gallbladder involvement).

Clinicians must review symptom logs at each refill or dose change.

3. When to adjust or stop dosing

WHO recommends dose adjustment or tapering when any of the following occurs:

  • Unintentional rapid weight loss over 15% within 60–90 days.
  • Recurring GI distress that interrupts food intake.
  • Liver or pancreatic lab abnormalities.

Stopping instantly is discouraged — tapering is now standard.

4. Off-label restrictions (2026)

WHO requires prescribers to document medical need for GLP-1 drugs. Cosmetic-only use without monitoring is discouraged.

Clinics must justify every prescription and maintain compliance logs.

5. What patients must do immediately

  • Start a baseline symptom, hydration & weight log.
  • Use WHO’s 2026 weekly monitoring checklist.
  • Report all red-flag symptoms within 24–48 hours.
  • Verify your treatment meets updated medical-need criteria.

2026 GLP-1 – Frequently Asked Questions

Does this change how Ozempic/Wegovy are prescribed?

Yes — monitoring rules and medical-need requirements are stricter.

Does guidance apply to non-diabetic users?

Yes. WHO applies these standards to all GLP-1 therapies.

Should I stop my medication?

No. Tapering with supervision is required — never stop abruptly.

Will insurance coverage change?

Insurers may adjust criteria to match WHO standards.
