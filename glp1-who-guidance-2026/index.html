Executive overview

1. What changed in the WHO 2026 GLP-1 advisory

The WHO GLP-1 Global Risk Advisory tightens controls on semaglutide, tirzepatide and related GLP-1 formulations across four axes: prescribing pathways, documentation, supply-chain integrity and public claims. Regulators, insurers and platforms are already aligning their audits with this framework.

  • Mandatory documentation of medical necessity and eligibility.
  • Explicit off-label justification and written informed consent.
  • Full supply-chain and cold-chain traceability by batch.
  • Advertising and claims restricted to non-promissory language.
  • Adverse-event logging and escalation pathways.

The advisory explicitly references clinics, tele-health platforms, pharmacies, compounding facilities, distributors and independent prescribers. All need auditable documentation ‚Äì not just ‚Äúgood intentions‚Äù.

At minimum, your files should include:

Off-label & informed consent

4. Off-label usage requirements (weight-loss & beyond)

Off-label prescribing remains legal in many jurisdictions, but undocumented off-label GLP-1 use is now a top audit trigger. Your records should clearly show:

  • Clinical rationale for off-label use (e.g. obesity with comorbidities).
  • Alternatives discussed and why GLP-1 was selected.
  • Documented patient expectations and limitations of evidence.
  • Signed off-label informed consent, stored for at least five years.
i
Many enforcement actions target missing paperwork, not just inappropriate use. A well-structured consent + rationale file is your first defence.
Supply-chain & cold-chain

5. Supply-chain verification & cold-chain integrity

WHO highlights four high-risk clusters: counterfeit, incorrect potency, misuse and cold-chain breaks.

  • Verify supplier licences, accreditations and COAs for each batch.
  • Record batch/lot numbers across prescribing, dispensing and inventory.
  • Maintain 24/7 temperature logs for storage and transport.
  • Quarantine and document any deviation outside the approved range.
  • Keep written procedures for destruction / return of suspect product.

Cold-chain log essentials

  • Batch ID and product description.
  • Time-stamped temperature readings.
  • Responsible staff initials.
  • Deviation notes + action taken.
Marketing & claims

6. GLP-1 marketing & claims compliance

Audits & response

7. Audit red flags & emergency response planning

Common triggers for regulatory or payer audits include:

  • Rapid patient-count growth without matching documentation.
  • High reliance on compounded products with limited COAs.
  • Missing or inconsistent cold-chain logs.
  • Non-standard sourcing or cross-border batches.
  • Promotional claims that diverge from product labelling.

When an audit notice arrives, your plan should:

  • Freeze questionable batches immediately.
  • Assemble eligibility, consent, COAs and temperature logs.
  • Assign a single compliance lead as contact point.
  • Respond within required time frames with complete documentation.
  • Pause aggressive marketing until the review is closed.
