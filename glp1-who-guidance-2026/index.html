Overview

1. What WHO changed in 2026

The 2026 WHO guidance introduces stricter GLP-1 safety rules due to rapid global use and rising reports of pancreatitis, gallbladder complications and severe gastrointestinal reactions. Key updates require:

  • mandatory baseline evaluation before prescription,
  • structured symptom monitoring at every refill,
  • faster reporting of red-flag reactions,
  • new tapering rules (no abrupt stopping),
  • clear documentation of medical necessity for all patients ‚Äî including weight-loss users.
!
WHO guidance applies to all GLP-1 formulations: Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro, Zepbound, Saxenda and their generics.
Safety Monitoring

2. New mandatory symptom monitoring requirements

WHO now requires a structured review of symptoms at every dose increase and refill. Patients must track:

  • upper abdominal pain radiating to the back (pancreatitis risk),
  • persistent nausea/vomiting beyond dose-change expectations,
  • severe bloating or distension,
  • right-upper-quadrant pain (gallbladder complications),
  • unintended rapid weight loss (>15% in 60‚Äì90 days).

WHO 2026 monitoring requirements

  • Baseline symptom and weight log before treatment.
  • Weekly GI symptom log.
  • Monthly weight and side-effect review.
  • Mandatory reporting of red-flag symptoms within 24‚Äì48h.
  • Lab monitoring: liver enzymes, pancreatic markers if symptoms appear.
Medication Adjustments

3. When to adjust or stop GLP-1 treatment

WHO defines clear thresholds where doctors must re-evaluate dosing:

  • rapid unintended weight loss >15% in under 3 months,
  • recurrent severe gastrointestinal events,
  • elevated pancreatic or liver markers,
  • new gallbladder-related symptoms.

Abrupt stopping is no longer recommended. WHO requires supervised tapering to reduce rebound appetite and adverse reactions.

Off-Label Use

4. Off-label use restrictions in 2026

WHO now states that GLP-1 prescriptions must include written medical justification. This affects:

  • cosmetic/rapid-slimming use without clinical supervision,
  • anti-craving or off-label psychiatric use,
  • use for mild overweight without comorbidities.

Clinics must keep documentation of:

  • baseline assessment,
  • treatment rationale,
  • monitoring notes,
  • risk discussion and patient consent.
