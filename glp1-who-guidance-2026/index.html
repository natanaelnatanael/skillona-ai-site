Overview

1. What changed in the WHO 2026 GLP-1 advisory

The advisory focuses on four global risk clusters that have exploded with mass GLP-1 adoption:

  • Counterfeit & grey-market product from online and cross-border channels.
  • Contaminated / wrong-potency batches, especially in non-accredited compounding.
  • Aggressive off-label expansion without documentation or consent.
  • Breaks in cold-chain integrity with no logs or deviation handling.

The result: more audits, payer reviews and licence scrutiny for any GLP-1 prescriber, clinic, pharmacy or compounding partner.

Off-label & consent

3. Off-label GLP-1 use ‚Äì new 2026 expectations

Off-label GLP-1 prescribing remains legal in many jurisdictions, but it is now a primary audit trigger. The advisory expects you to:

  • Record a clear, written off-label rationale for every patient.
  • Obtain signed informed consent with alternatives explained.
  • Document expectations, monitoring schedule and risk profile.
  • Store this documentation for a minimum of five years.

Cold chain & supply

4. Cold-chain and supplier verification

Breaks in storage and weak supplier checks are now viewed as systemic risk, not minor operational issues. You are expected to:

  • Run continuous temperature monitoring with 24/7 logs.
  • Quarantine and document any cold-chain deviation.
  • Collect and file COAs and batch testing reports.
  • Verify licences and accreditations for all suppliers and compounders.

Included operational templates

  • Cold-chain log (batch ID, timestamp, temperature, staff initials).
  • Deviation procedure checklist.
  • Supplier verification form.
  • Compounded product verification template.
Adverse events & audits

5. Adverse event reporting and audit preparation

The advisory expects structured adverse-event capture and escalation, plus a defined emergency response if you receive an audit notice.

  • Standardised event form (type, severity, batch, timing, action taken).
  • Clear routing: manufacturer, regulator, WHO / national body.
  • Audit red-flags: undocumented off-label use, missing cold-chain logs, high compounder volume, no COAs.

The pack includes a one-page emergency response plan so staff know exactly what to freeze, pull and send within the audit timeframe.
