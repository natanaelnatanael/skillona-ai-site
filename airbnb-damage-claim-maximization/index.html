Foundation

1. What AirCover actually is (and isn‚Äôt)

Airbnb‚Äôs AirCover for Hosts is a set of policies that can reimburse you for certain guest-caused damage and extra costs. It is not a traditional insurance policy and Airbnb has wide discretion to approve or deny claims for your vacation rental or short-term rental unit.

Typical categories that may be covered include:

  • Physical damage to your property or contents caused by guests.
  • Extra cleaning and odor removal after severe misuse or parties.
  • Lost income for cancelled nights due to major damage (case by case).
  • Rekeying locks, replacing damaged linens, furniture, electronics, etc.

Airbnb will look at: clear evidence, timelines, your communication with the guest and whether the cost is reasonable and properly documented for each item.

2. Timelines: the biggest reason hosts lose money

Airbnb has strict timing rules that change from time to time, but they always include:

  • A deadline to start your claim after checkout (often 14 days or next guest).
  • A deadline to upload all evidence and receipts for your damages.
  • A requirement to contact the guest through Airbnb first.

If you miss the timeline, even perfect evidence can be denied with ‚Äúoutside our timeframe‚Äù and there is usually no appeal.

Damage-day checklist (same day the guest leaves)

  • Walkthrough with camera before cleaners touch anything.
  • Take photos / video of every damage, mess and evidence of rule-breaking.
  • Screenshot booking page with guest details and dates.
  • Message the guest politely about the issue inside Airbnb.
  • Open a claim in the Resolution Center within the allowed window.

Step-by-step claim

4. Exact claim workflow (from discovery to payout)

  1. Inspect immediately after checkout. Do not let cleaners ‚Äúfix everything‚Äù before you document it.
  2. Document damage. Photos, videos, notes, cleaner messages.
  3. Message the guest via Airbnb. Stay calm, factual, and ask them to confirm what happened.
  4. Open a claim in the Resolution Center. Attach photos and a clear cost breakdown.
  5. If the guest accepts, Airbnb charges their payment method.
  6. If the guest ignores or refuses, escalate to AirCover support within the required timeframe.
  7. Respond fast to any follow-up questions and upload extra documents they request.

Cost & pricing

6. How to calculate and justify your claim amount

Airbnb prefers realistic, documented costs over round numbers. To keep your claim credible:

  • Use actual invoices where possible, not guesses.
  • If you can‚Äôt get an invoice quickly, use screenshots of comparable items with links.
  • For your own labour, be conservative and show an hourly rate that matches local cleaners / handymen.
  • Spell out each item separately instead of ‚Äúdamage ‚Äì $800‚Äù.

Example breakdown that reads well to reps

  • Sofa steam-cleaning (professional invoice attached): $120
  • Replacement lamp (link attached): $65
  • Extra cleaner time ‚Äì 2 hours @ $25/h (timesheet attached): $50
  • Total requested: $235

Prevention

8. House rules and setup that protect your claims

Strong claims start before the guest arrives. Airbnb checks if your rules and expectations were clear.

  • Put key rules in House Rules + listing description (no parties, no smoking, pet policy, max guests).
  • Use a short pre-arrival message that repeats the most important rules.
  • Keep fragile / irreplaceable items out of the listing altogether.
  • Have a simple inventory list for cleaners (so new damage is spotted quickly).

9. When to involve local police or security

In serious cases (major vandalism, theft, violence), Airbnb often wants a police report or at least a reference number. Consider calling local authorities when:

  • There is clear deliberate vandalism or large-scale theft.
  • Drugs, weapons or violence are involved.
  • Neighbours were disturbed and there is ongoing risk to safety.

Do this first for safety reasons; the report then becomes part of your claim package.

Mistakes & FAQs

10. Common mistakes that kill Airbnb damage claims

  • Cleaning everything before you take photos (‚Äúwe already fixed it‚Äù).
  • Waiting days to open a claim or contact the guest.
  • Discussing damage only via phone or WhatsApp, not in the Airbnb message thread.
  • Submitting one blurry photo and no receipts, then expecting full compensation.
  • Claiming for wear and tear (old sofa, faded towels) as if it was fresh damage.

11. Quick FAQs

Is every mess or broken item covered?
No. AirCover is aimed at significant damage, not normal wear and tear. A broken plate is different from a destroyed TV or heavily stained, ruined mattress.
Do I need professional invoices or can I DIY?
You can claim for your own labour, but professional invoices are usually viewed as stronger. If you DIY, be realistic about your hourly rate and show receipts for materials.
Can I still leave an honest review if I claim?
Yes. You should leave a factual, calm review so other hosts know what happened. Avoid insults ‚Äì stick to behaviour and damage.
Will Airbnb punish me for filing ‚Äútoo many‚Äù claims?
Frequent claims may draw more scrutiny, but if your evidence is strong and you only claim for real damage, there is no rule that you can only claim a certain number of times.
