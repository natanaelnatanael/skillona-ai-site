Basics

1. Can you open a U.S. bank account without an SSN?

For a business (LLC, corporation, partnership), banks focus on the company‚Äôs details and the owners‚Äô identity ‚Äì not only on Social Security Numbers.

Many traditional banks still prefer at least one owner with an SSN and physical U.S. presence. But in 2025, there are realistic paths for foreign owners to get a U.S. business account using:

  • An EIN (Employer Identification Number) for the LLC.
  • Passport and proof of address (foreign accepted by some providers).
  • Company formation documents (Articles, Operating Agreement).
!
You cannot force a bank to accept you. Each institution has its own risk policy and can reject a foreign LLC without giving a detailed reason. The strategy is to apply where your profile actually fits.

2. Core documents U.S. banks usually want

Expect to provide some or all of the following:

  • Company EIN letter from the IRS.
  • Articles of Organization / Incorporation (e.g. Wyoming, Delaware).
  • Operating Agreement or similar document listing members.
  • Passport for each ultimate owner / manager.
  • Proof of address (can be foreign or U.S. depending on bank).
  • Description of business activity and main countries you deal with.

Bank-ready LLC checklist

  • LLC formed (Wyoming, Delaware, etc.).
  • EIN obtained and IRS letter saved as PDF.
  • Operating Agreement clearly showing ownership percentages.
  • Dedicated business email + U.S. mailing address or mail service.
Provider types

3. Types of providers that work with foreign LLCs

In practice you have three broad categories:

3.1. Online fintech business accounts

These are tech-first platforms that partner with underlying U.S. banks. Many are willing to onboard non-U.S. founders if:

  • Company is U.S.-registered and has an EIN.
  • Owners pass identity and sanctions checks.
  • Business is low-risk (no crypto mixing, gambling, etc.).

Pros: remote onboarding, modern interface, integrations, virtual and physical debit cards, often no monthly fee.

Cons: stricter on sanctions / country lists, sudden account reviews.

3.2. Traditional U.S. brick-and-mortar banks

Large and regional banks (Chase, Bank of America, etc.) can work, but typically require:

  • At least one visit to a branch in person.
  • Stronger preference for a U.S. address and contact number.
  • Sometimes an SSN or ITIN for at least one signer.

Pros: perceived stability, physical branches, easier cash deposits.

Cons: travel needed, more paperwork, higher probability of ‚Äúno‚Äù for fully foreign-owned structures.

3.3. Specialized ‚Äúinternational founder‚Äù services

Some providers bundle LLC formation + EIN + bank/fintech account setup specifically targeted at non-resident founders.

Pros: one-stop shop, they know which banks accept foreigners right now, less trial and error.

Cons: service fees; you must verify how they handle your data and who the underlying bank is.

Practical steps

4. Step-by-step plan to get a U.S. account as a foreign LLC owner

  1. Form the LLC in a business-friendly state (Wyoming, Delaware, etc.) and keep all formation documents.
  2. Obtain an EIN from the IRS ‚Äì even if you do not have an SSN (foreign owners can still get an EIN with the right form).
  3. Prepare a clear Operating Agreement with owner names, percentages and management structure.
  4. Choose 2‚Äì3 providers that explicitly state they accept non-resident founders.
  5. Apply with full and honest information about owners, source of funds, countries, and expected transaction volumes.
  6. Once approved, keep activity consistent with what you told them to avoid compliance reviews.

Your best approval chances: simple business model (e.g. consulting, digital products, SaaS, property holding), clean countries, and transparent ownership ‚Äì not complex offshore chains.

5. What about ITIN ‚Äì is it required?

For a business account, banks mainly rely on EIN plus personal identity documents. An ITIN can help but is often not strictly required if:

  • The account is clearly a business account, and
  • You are not trying to open a personal U.S. checking account.

However, for tax reporting (K-1s, 1040-NR, FIRPTA), having an ITIN as a foreign owner is usually recommended. You can:

  • Open the account first using EIN + passport.
  • Then obtain an ITIN using your first U.S. tax filing.
Risk & compliance

6. Why foreign-owned LLC accounts are often flagged

Banks see foreign-owned entities as higher risk for regulatory reasons (KYC/AML, sanctions, tax reporting). Common triggers:

  • Frequent international wires from or to high-risk countries.
  • Crypto exchange activity through a business account.
  • Large spikes in volume inconsistent with your business profile.
  • Missing or outdated KYC documents when they request updates.

Result can be: account review, temporary freeze, or termination with a mailed check for remaining balance.

7. How to keep the account stable

  • Answer KYC emails quickly and completely.
  • Keep proof of source of funds (invoices, contracts, leases).
  • Avoid using the business account as your personal wallet.
  • Use a clean, professional domain email (not free webmail only).
  • Stay away from prohibited activities in the provider‚Äôs terms.
Mistakes & FAQs

8. Common mistakes foreign LLC owners make

  • Applying to random big-name banks that clearly state they do not onboard non-resident entities.
  • Using mail forwarding addresses that are on internal ‚Äúflagged‚Äù lists.
  • Hiding beneficial owners or using nominee structures that look suspicious.
  • Mixing investment, personal and client funds in one account.

9. Quick FAQs

Can I open a U.S. bank account fully online?
With some fintech providers, yes ‚Äì if your LLC and documents meet their criteria. Traditional banks usually still require in-person visits for foreign owners.
Do I need a U.S. address?
Many providers accept a business mail address or virtual office for mailing, but they will still record your foreign residential address for KYC. Check each provider‚Äôs rules.
What if my account is closed?
You usually receive remaining funds via check or wire. Use the experience to choose a more suitable provider and ensure your documents and business profile are cleaner the next time.
