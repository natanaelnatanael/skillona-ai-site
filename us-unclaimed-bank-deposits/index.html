1. What Are Unclaimed Bank Deposits?

Unclaimed bank deposits are funds left inactive for several years—usually 3 to 5—after which banks must transfer them to the state’s official unclaimed property program.

Types of funds that become unclaimed:

  • Checking and savings accounts
  • Certificates of deposit (CDs)
  • Uncashed cashier’s checks
  • Closed accounts with residual balances
Why States Hold These Funds

States act only as custodians—the money still belongs to you and can be claimed at any time.

2. Who Can Claim These Deposits?

  • The original account holder
  • Heirs or estate representatives
  • Businesses that had accounts in their name
  • Authorized legal representatives

3. How to Search for Unclaimed Bank Funds

Start with the official nationwide search:

  • unclaimed.org (free, official)

Then check individual state sites, especially where you lived, worked, or opened accounts.

4. How to Claim Unclaimed Bank Deposits (Step-by-Step)

  1. Search your name on the state's unclaimed property website.
  2. Confirm the match by address or institution name.
  3. Submit an online claim.
  4. Upload required documents (ID, proof of address, bank docs).
  5. Wait for approval and receive funds by check or deposit.
Tip

Always submit clear scans of identification to avoid delays.

5. Common Claiming Mistakes

  • Not checking all states
  • Using unofficial paid websites
  • Submitting unclear or incomplete documents
  • Ignoring mailed verification requests

6. Can Foreign Nationals File Claims?

Yes. You only need:

  • Passport or national ID
  • Proof you once owned or were entitled to the account
