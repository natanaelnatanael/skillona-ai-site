How to Claim Unclaimed Bank Deposits in the US (2025 Guide)

1. What Are Unclaimed Bank Deposits?

Unclaimed bank deposits refer to funds that have been left untouched for an extended period. These can include dormant accounts, uncashed checks, or other forms of bank balances that have not been claimed by the rightful owners.

Banks and financial institutions are legally required to turn over these unclaimed funds to the state after a certain period, typically 3-5 years, depending on the state’s laws.

Why Claim Unclaimed Deposits?

If you have unclaimed funds in a bank account or through other financial institutions, you are entitled to retrieve it. The state acts as a custodian, and claiming your funds is your legal right.

2. Who Can Claim Unclaimed Bank Deposits?

You can claim unclaimed bank deposits if you are the rightful owner, which can include:

  • Primary account holders
  • Heirs or beneficiaries (with proper documentation)
  • Legal representatives or attorneys

Even if you are not a current account holder, but the account was under your name previously, you may still be eligible to claim it.

3. How to Find Unclaimed Bank Deposits

To locate unclaimed bank deposits, you can search state databases. Every state in the US maintains a free public database where you can search for unclaimed property by name.

  • Visit the National Association of Unclaimed Property Administrators website.
  • Use the state-specific websites to search for your name or business name.

You’ll need to provide your name, address, and possibly other personal information to confirm that the funds belong to you.

4. Step-by-Step Process to Claim Unclaimed Bank Deposits

Here’s how you can claim your unclaimed bank deposits:

  1. Search the state database for any unclaimed deposits under your name.
  2. Submit a claim by filling out the online form on the state’s unclaimed property site.
  3. Provide supporting documentation (proof of identity, ownership, etc.) if required.
  4. Wait for confirmation from the state, usually within 6-12 weeks.
  5. Receive your funds via check, direct deposit, or another payment method chosen by the state.
Tip

Make sure you keep a copy of all documents submitted and receive confirmation of your claim. If your claim is denied, you can often appeal the decision.

5. Common Mistakes to Avoid When Claiming Unclaimed Deposits

  • Not checking all states – you may have accounts in multiple states.
  • Missing claim deadlines – make sure to file claims promptly to avoid forfeiting your funds.
  • Failing to provide adequate documentation – ensure you provide clear proof of your identity and ownership.

6. How Long Does It Take to Claim Unclaimed Deposits?

The process typically takes between 6 to 12 weeks, depending on the state. You will be notified once your claim has been processed.

The claim process can vary slightly depending on the state’s rules and the complexity of the claim.

7. Can Foreign Nationals Claim Unclaimed Bank Deposits?

Yes, foreign nationals can claim unclaimed bank deposits in the US, as long as they have proper documentation proving their identity and ownership of the funds.

You will need to provide personal identification documents, such as a passport, to prove your identity.
