1. What Are IRS Tax Liens and Surplus Funds?

IRS tax liens occur when the Internal Revenue Service places a claim on a taxpayer’s property for unpaid taxes. If the property is sold at auction to satisfy the lien, any excess proceeds from the sale are called surplus funds. These funds are held by the government for the rightful owner or other claimants.

For example, if the sale of the property covered the IRS lien and there is a surplus, that excess amount belongs to the previous owner or other legal claimants.

Key Information

Surplus funds are legally owed to the original property owner or other entitled parties. To claim them, you must file an official claim with the IRS or the agency responsible for holding the funds.

2. Who Can Claim Surplus Funds from IRS Tax Liens?

Eligible claimants may include:

  • The property owner at the time the IRS lien was placed
  • Heirs or beneficiaries of a deceased property owner (with proper documentation)
  • Mortgage holders and other lienholders recorded before the IRS lien

The IRS will release surplus funds only to individuals or entities that can prove their legal interest in the property.

3. How to Find Surplus Funds from IRS Tax Liens

The IRS maintains records of tax lien auctions and surplus funds. Here’s how to find out if you have surplus funds:

  • Visit the IRS website or the county website where the lien sale occurred.
  • Search for the property auction results or contact the appropriate office.
  • You’ll need the tax lien number, property address, or parcel number to search for your surplus funds.

4. How to Claim Surplus Funds from IRS Tax Liens

Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to claim your surplus funds:

  1. Locate the surplus funds by searching the IRS or county auction records for any available funds.
  2. Gather the necessary documentation, such as proof of ownership, legal interest, or heirship.
  3. Submit the claim form to the IRS or appropriate office handling the surplus funds.
  4. Provide identification and any supporting documents to prove your claim.
  5. Wait for approval – once the claim is processed, you will be notified if your claim is successful.
  6. Receive your funds via check or wire transfer, depending on the IRS guidelines.
Tip

Always keep a copy of the claim form and supporting documents. Follow up with the IRS if you do not receive a response within the expected timeline.

5. Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Submitting incomplete claim forms
  • Failing to provide necessary documentation
  • Missing deadlines for filing claims
  • Not following up on your claim after submission

6. How Long Does It Take to Get Your Surplus Funds?

The process for receiving your surplus funds can take anywhere from 30 days to several months, depending on the complexity of your case and the IRS processing times.

If your claim is approved, funds will typically be disbursed through check or bank transfer.
