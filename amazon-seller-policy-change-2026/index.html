1. Overview of Amazon’s 2026 seller policy changes

Amazon’s 2026 policy updates were introduced to reduce counterfeit items, improve listing accuracy and ensure sellers can verify product authenticity. These changes apply to all sellers — FBA, FBM, private label, wholesale and arbitrage.

Key changes include:

  • Stricter identity verification requirements
  • New invoice validation rules
  • AI-generated listing detection and penalties
  • Stronger enforcement against duplicate listings
  • Revised A-to-Z guarantee and return dispute rules
  • Proof-of-origin documentation for select categories
Amazon’s 2026 policy enforcement is more automated, and suspension appeal windows are shorter — meaning mistakes must be prevented, not fixed later.

2. New identity & verification requirements

Amazon now requires periodic re-verification of seller identity. This includes:

  • Updated government ID checks
  • Business registration verification
  • Bank account ownership verification
  • Video or live identity confirmation for high-risk sellers

Sellers operating from multiple countries or using third-party prep centers are under higher scrutiny.

3. Invoice, supplier & authenticity rules

Amazon now rejects invoices if they do not meet strict criteria. Requirements include:

  • Full supplier address and contact details
  • Matching seller name and address
  • Clear quantities and product descriptions
  • Verifiable suppliers — not brokers or unverifiable sources
  • No handwritten invoices

Amazon may directly contact suppliers to verify authenticity. If the supplier cannot confirm — Amazon assumes counterfeit → **ASIN suspension or account deactivation**.

4. Returns & refund policy changes for 2026

Key return-related updates:

  • Amazon now auto-refunds more return categories before sellers receive the item
  • New abuse detection system flags high return rates
  • More responsibility on sellers to prove buyer fault
  • New evidence upload requirements when sellers dispute returns

FBA sellers face additional warehouse evaluation rules for returned items.

5. AI-generated content enforcement

Amazon now identifies AI-generated:

  • Product titles
  • Bullet points
  • Descriptions
  • Feature claims

Listings that contain unverifiable or “too generic” language may be suppressed.

6. Higher account suspension triggers

Accounts are now suspended faster for:

  • Order defect rate trends (not just thresholds)
  • Repeated minor customer complaints
  • Invoice mismatches or unverifiable suppliers
  • Multiple ASIN listing violations
  • Delayed responses to Amazon compliance requests

Amazon now uses predictive risk modeling — meaning you can be suspended even before a major issue occurs.

7. Step-by-step compliance checklist

✔ Step 1 — Review all supplier invoices

  • Ensure address, phone and business registration match
  • Confirm supplier is verifiable

✔ Step 2 — Update listings

  • Remove exaggerated claims
  • Rewrite AI-generated content to look natural and verifiable

✔ Step 3 — Monitor account health daily

  • Watch for new alerts
  • Resolve warnings within 24 hours

✔ Step 4 — Document your supply chain

  • Save all invoices, emails, and supplier confirmations

✔ Step 5 — Prepare for re-verification

  • Ensure ID and business documents are current
  • Keep bank ownership information updated

FAQ (2026)

Do these changes affect arbitrage sellers?

Yes — arbitrage sellers face the highest risk due to stricter invoice verification rules.

Will Amazon ban AI completely?

No — but AI content must be rewritten and verified to avoid suppression signals.

Can I appeal a suspension under the new rules?

Yes — but Amazon now rejects more appeals that lack detailed root cause analysis and preventive actions.
