FAQ (2026)

1. What are the new IRS crypto reporting requirements for 2026?

Starting in 2026, the IRS requires all U.S. taxpayers to report all cryptocurrency transactions if they exceed $600 per year. This includes income from mining, staking, sales of crypto assets, and crypto-to-crypto exchanges. Transactions must be reported on Form 1040, Schedule D, and Form 8949, and taxpayers must also report the basis of their crypto assets, including the cost of acquisition and the sale proceeds.

2. What digital assets are included in the IRS crypto reporting rules?

Digital assets subject to reporting include:

  • Bitcoin, Ethereum, and all cryptocurrencies
  • Stablecoins like USDT, USDC
  • Non-fungible tokens (NFTs)
  • Any other tokenized digital assets

The IRS treats all of these assets as property, which means transactions involving them must be reported as capital gains or losses, and any income from digital assets must be reported as income.

3. How do I report my crypto transactions?

Crypto transactions must be reported on your 2026 tax return using:

  • Form 1040: The main tax form where all crypto-related income and deductions are reported.
  • Schedule D: For reporting capital gains and losses from crypto sales.
  • Form 8949: For reporting specific sales or exchanges of crypto, including detailed information like purchase and sale dates and amounts.

For crypto income such as staking rewards, these must be reported as regular income on the tax return.

Frequently Asked Questions (2026)

1. What are the new IRS crypto reporting requirements for 2026?

The IRS requires U.S. taxpayers to report crypto transactions over $600, including sales, exchanges, and crypto-based income. This includes income from mining and staking rewards, as well as capital gains from crypto sales or exchanges.

2. What digital assets must be reported?

All cryptocurrencies, stablecoins, NFTs, and tokenized assets must be reported. This includes assets like Bitcoin, Ethereum, and newer altcoins.

3. How do I report crypto transactions on my taxes?

Use IRS Form 1040, Schedule D, and Form 8949 to report all crypto-related transactions. Ensure you include any crypto-to-crypto exchanges, sales, and income from crypto activities like staking or mining.
